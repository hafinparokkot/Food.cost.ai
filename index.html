The Hidden Problem

Why Food Businesses Lose Money Without Knowing It

You calculated ingredient costs. You priced your product. You thought you'd profit. Then you didn't. Here's the hidden math most food businesses miss.

⚖️

Yield Loss Is Ignored

100 kg of raw banana becomes just 30–35 kg of chips. That 65–70% loss triples your effective raw material cost — yet most businesses price based on what they bought, not what they made.

📦

Packaging Costs Overlooked

Premium packaging runs ₹8–18 per unit. Across a 10,000 unit/month operation, that's ₹80,000–₹1.8L monthly — rarely factored into product pricing.

🔌

Utility Costs Untracked

Electricity, LPG, and water for frying and drying typically adds 8–12% to manufacturing cost. Most businesses estimate this wildly or ignore it entirely.

🚚

Distribution Stack Miscalculated

Distributor (10–15%) + Retailer (20–30%) + Tax (varies) = your retail price must be 45–65% above factory price just to break even. Most manufacturers underestimate this.

👷

Labor Cost Hidden

Manual peeling, sorting, and packaging labor is the #1 hidden cost in small food businesses. ₹5,000–₹25,000/month in labor never appears in ingredient cost calculations.

📊

No Profitability Visibility

Without a complete cost model, a 20% raw material price spike can silently destroy margins — and you won't realize it until you see the bank account at month end.
