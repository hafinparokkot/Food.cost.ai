How to Price Your Food Products

Pricing a food product requires accounting for the full distribution chain. A product that costs ₹14 to manufacture typically needs a retail price of 2×2.5× to remain profitable after distributor (10–15%) and retailer (20–30%) margins plus applicable taxes.

The Distribution Chain Explained

Factory → C&F Agent (3–5%) → Distributor (10–15%) → Retailer (20–30%) → Consumer (price inc. tax). Many small food businesses skip the C&F agent and sell directly to distributors. Modern trade (supermarkets, ecommerce) may have different margin structures but follow similar economics.
