Understanding Yield Loss in Food Manufacturing

Yield loss is the single most underestimated cost factor in food manufacturing. When 100 kg of raw banana becomes only 32 kg of finished chips, every overhead — oil, electricity, labor, packaging — must be calculated against those 32 kg, not the original 100 kg. This 3× cost multiplier is why many food businesses price their products incorrectly and lose money without realizing it.

Banana Chips: Real Yield Data from India

According to NIFTEM research and KVIC project reports, raw Nendran banana yields approximately 30–35% as finished chips. Peeling accounts for 40–50% of the loss, with the remainder from slicing wastage and moisture loss during frying. You need 3–3.5 kg of raw banana to produce 1 kg of banana chips.
